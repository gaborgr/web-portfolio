About Me

My name is Gabriel Guerra. I’m 42 years old, originally from Venezuela, but I’ve been living in Chile since 2014. My decision to relocate was driven by a desire for growth—both professionally and personally—amid challenging circumstances in my home country. I made this choice with my future and that of my loved ones in mind, and I’m grateful to say it has been a rewarding journey. Adapting to a new culture and environment has shaped me, but my innate curiosity and drive continue to push me toward new horizons and challenges.

I believe growth comes from stepping out of our comfort zones, and I’m always eager to test my abilities and unlock my full potential. Every challenge I face is an opportunity to prove to myself that success can be achieved in any setting with determination, resilience, and an open mind.

On a personal note, I have a wonderful family. I’m married, and while we don’t have children yet, it’s part of our plans. I enjoy spending quality time with my family and friends, traveling, meeting new people, and immersing myself in different cultures. I believe these experiences enrich my perspective and contribute to my personal and professional growth.

While I’m proud of what I’ve achieved so far, I know there’s still room to reach greater heights. This is why I continuously seek new challenges that not only add value to my life but also enhance the lives of those around me.

Throughout my career, I’ve had the privilege of working with various companies, which has given me the invaluable skill of perspective. This allows me to approach situations with an open mind, considering diverse viewpoints to make informed decisions that align with my goals and contribute to positive outcomes.

As a person, I’m open to new ideas, tolerant, and genuinely curious about understanding others—not just from their point of view but also by exploring the intentions behind their opinions.

Living abroad for these past years has taught me the importance of hard work and perseverance. It’s been a journey of learning, growth, and self-discovery, and I know the best is yet to come. This is why I’m seeking a new challenge—one that aligns with my skills, aspirations, and passion for continuous improvement.

I hope this brief introduction gives you a sense of who I am and what I can bring to the table. I look forward to the opportunity to discuss in more detail how my experiences, both professional and personal, can contribute to your team’s success.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Best regards, Gabriel Guerra
